Draft — pending UK solicitor review. This policy has been prepared against UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018 and the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) using current product behaviour. It must be reviewed by a qualified UK data-protection adviser before public launch.

This Privacy Policy explains how LOGDit ("we", "us", "our") collects, uses, stores and protects your information when you use our mobile application and related services (the "Service"). We are committed to handling your data in line with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

In short: we redact patient identifiers from your transcript before it is used to draft your entry, store data on UK and EEA infrastructure, never use your recordings to train AI models, and let you delete anything at any time.

LOGDit is operated by LOGDIT LTD (trading as "LOGDit"), a company registered in England and Wales (company number 17215461), registered office: 39 Wallington Way, Frome BA11 3NF, United Kingdom. We are the data controller for personal data described in this policy.

For all privacy matters, including data subject requests and complaints, please contact privacy@logdit.app.

2. What we collect

Account information you provide

  • Email address (used as your account identifier and to send sign-in codes)
  • Display name (optional)
  • Medical specialty (e.g. General Practice) and training stage (e.g. ST1, ST3) — optional, used to load the right curriculum framework
  • A one-time passcode (OTP) we email to you to verify sign-in (deleted automatically after 5 minutes)

Content you create in the app

  • Voice recordings you make when logging a case
  • Transcripts derived from those recordings, after patient identifiers have been redacted (see section 6)
  • AI-generated draft reflections, capability tags and PDP suggestions, plus any edits and version history you create
  • Free-text replies you send in the in-app conversation

Technical and device data we collect automatically

  • App version, device type and operating system (sent with each request)
  • Session identifiers and approximate device label (e.g. "iPhone, iOS 18") for managing your signed-in devices
  • IP address (used briefly for security, rate-limiting and abuse prevention)
  • Crash reports and error diagnostics via our error-monitoring provider (see section 8). We do not send your name, email, content or authentication tokens to that provider.

What we do not collect

We do not knowingly collect identifiable patient data. Our pipeline removes patient identifiers (such as names, dates of birth, NHS numbers, addresses and postcodes) from transcripts before they are used to draft your portfolio entry. See section 6 for the full pipeline, including which sub-processors handle your audio and transcript at each stage. We do not collect your location, contacts, photos, calendar, browser history or advertising identifiers. We do not use third-party advertising or cross-site tracking.

3. Microphone and device permissions

The app asks for permission to use your microphone so you can record a voice note. Recording only happens when you tap to record. We do not record in the background. You can revoke microphone permission at any time in your device settings; you will then be unable to use the voice recording feature.

4. How we use your information

  • To provide and operate the Service (transcription, redaction, drafting entries, exporting PDFs)
  • To create and authenticate your account, including sending OTP sign-in codes
  • To improve the Service (aggregated and de-identified usage analysis, crash diagnostics, model-output quality checks)
  • To communicate service updates, security notices and (with your consent) product news
  • To detect, prevent and respond to fraud, abuse or security incidents
  • To comply with legal obligations and respond to lawful requests

5. Legal bases for processing (UK GDPR Article 6)

  • Contract — to deliver the Service to you under our Terms of Service (account creation, authentication, transcription, drafting, storage of your entries).
  • Legitimate interests — to keep the Service secure, prevent fraud, monitor errors and improve the product. We balance these interests against your rights and freedoms; you can object at any time (see section 11).
  • Consent — for optional marketing communications and any feature you explicitly opt into. You can withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of earlier processing.
  • Legal obligation — where we are required to retain or disclose information by applicable law.

Special category data (UK GDPR Article 9)

Your reflections may relate to your professional development as a healthcare professional and could, in context, amount to "data concerning health" under Article 9 of the UK GDPR. Where that is the case, our additional lawful condition is Article 9(2)(h) — health and social care purposes, as enacted in UK law by Schedule 1, Part 1, paragraph 2 of the Data Protection Act 2018 (and specifically paragraph 2(2)(f), "the management of health care systems or services"). LOGDit supports UK trainee doctors in producing the reflective practice entries that the General Medical Council (GMC) and training programmes require for revalidation and workforce development — purposes that fall within the management of the UK health care system.

We rely on Article 9(2)(h) rather than your explicit consent because AI transcription, redaction and drafting are necessary to deliver the Service and cannot be turned off. Under UK GDPR Article 7, consent must be "freely given"; consent obtained as a precondition of using a service that cannot operate without that processing is not freely given, and would not be a valid basis here.

The Article 9(3) professional-secrecy requirement is satisfied because you, as a UK trainee doctor using LOGDit, are bound by the General Medical Council's duty of confidentiality, and we operate under written confidentiality and data-protection obligations under our Terms of Service and the Data Protection Act 2018.

If you do not wish your data to be processed in this way, please do not create an account, or close your account at any time. Closing your account (UK GDPR Article 17 erasure) is the mechanism by which you withdraw from this processing; there is no separate "turn off AI" option because the Service cannot operate without AI transcription, redaction and drafting.

6. PII redaction and AI processing

Your reflection is processed by external speech-to-text and language-model sub-processors (listed on our sub-processors page) to produce a transcript, redact patient identifiers and draft your portfolio entry.

Before any text is used to draft your entry, it passes through automated redaction that removes both structured identifiers (NHS numbers, National Insurance numbers, email addresses, UK phone numbers, UK postcodes, payment card numbers and labelled dates of birth) and contextual identifiers (personal names, organisation names and place names that appear alongside specific persons).

What this means for your data. Because transcription and redaction are themselves automated processes, your audio and the raw transcript briefly transit our sub-processors in unredacted form before the redacted version is produced. The audio recording itself is only sent to the speech-to-text processor; it is never sent to a language model. Our sub-processors operate under Data Processing Agreements that prohibit them from using your content for any other purpose (including AI model training) and require them to retain it only for the time needed to return a result. Audio is automatically deleted from our storage within 72 hours of transcription (see section 7).

Automated decision-making (UK GDPR Article 22)

Our AI generates draft reflections, suggested capability tags and proposed PDP goals. These are drafts only. They have no legal or similarly significant effect on you. Nothing is submitted to your eportfolio, training programme director or deanery automatically. You review, edit and approve every entry before exporting or using it. You can always start from a blank entry and ignore the AI suggestions.

7. Data storage and retention

  • Personal data is stored on cloud infrastructure in the United Kingdom and the European Economic Area, encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest. Specific storage locations and providers are listed on our sub-processors page (see section 8).
  • Audio recordings are stored encrypted and automatically deleted within 72 hours of transcription.
  • Transcripts, reflections, capability tags and PDP goals are retained while your account is active. You can delete any individual entry at any time from inside the app.
  • One-time sign-in codes (OTPs) expire and are deleted after 5 minutes.
  • Sign-in sessions expire after 90 days of inactivity and are then deleted.
  • Account closure — when you request account deletion there is a 30-day grace period during which you can cancel. After that, all your content is permanently deleted and your account record is anonymised, except where we are required to retain limited records (for example, for accounting, tax or legal dispute purposes) for the period set by law.
  • Crash and error logs are retained by our error-monitoring provider for up to 90 days then deleted.

8. Sub-processors

We use a small set of trusted third-party providers to operate the Service. These include providers of cloud compute and hosting, database services, object storage, speech-to-text transcription, large language model processing, business email and mailbox services, and application error monitoring.

Each sub-processor is bound by a written data processing agreement (in most cases auto-incorporated by reference into the provider's terms of service) that restricts use of your personal data to the purposes for which we engage them. Our sub-processors are contractually prohibited from using your content to train AI or machine learning models.

A current, named list of our sub-processors — with their roles, the data they process, regions and applicable international-transfer mechanisms — is published at logdit.app/sub-processors. We will give reasonable advance notice on that page of material changes (such as adding a new sub-processor or changing the role of an existing one) before they take effect, and where practicable will notify active users by email or in-app.

Our Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) summary is available on request at privacy@logdit.app.

9. International transfers

Most processing happens in the United Kingdom and the European Economic Area, both of which provide an adequate level of protection for personal data under UK law (UK→EEA adequacy regulations 2021). Where personal data is transferred outside the UK and EEA — specifically, to OpenAI, L.L.C. in the United States for language-model processing — we rely on the EU Standard Contractual Clauses with the UK Addendum as our transfer mechanism, as incorporated into OpenAI's standard Data Processing Addendum. Supplementary safeguards include contractual restrictions on use of your content for AI model training, contractual limits on retention to what is needed to return a result, and our internal multi-stage redaction pipeline (see section 6), which removes patient identifiers from your transcript before it is used to draft your entry. Note that the audio, raw transcript and cleaned transcript may transit our processors in unredacted form during transcription and cleaning, as described in section 6.

A summary of our Transfer Impact Assessment is available on request at privacy@logdit.app.

10. Sharing your data

We only share personal data with:

  • Our sub-processors, as listed in section 8;
  • Professional advisers (lawyers, auditors, insurers) under a duty of confidentiality, where strictly necessary;
  • Public authorities, where required by law, court order or legitimate regulatory process;
  • A buyer or successor entity, in the event of a merger, acquisition, restructuring or sale of assets, with notice to you.

We do not sell your personal data. We do not share your content with your training programme director, supervisor, deanery, employer, the GMC or any other third party unless you choose to export and share it yourself.

11. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you (Art. 15)
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete data (Art. 16)
  • Request deletion of your data — the "right to be forgotten" (Art. 17)
  • Restrict or object to certain processing (Art. 18 and Art. 21)
  • Receive a portable export of your data (Art. 20)
  • Withdraw consent at any time, where consent is the legal basis (Art. 7)
  • Not be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing that produce legal or similarly significant effects (Art. 22) — see section 6 on how this applies.
  • Complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office at ico.org.uk or by calling 0303 123 1113.

To exercise any of these rights, email privacy@logdit.app. We respond within one calendar month, in line with UK GDPR.

12. Security

We use measures appropriate to the risk of processing, including TLS encryption in transit, AES-256 encryption at rest, role-based access controls, audit logging of administrative actions, separate environments for development and production, secret management for credentials, and regular review of our sub-processors. Sign-in tokens are stored in your device's secure keychain (iOS Keychain / Android Keystore), not in plain storage. No system is perfectly secure; if you believe your account has been compromised, contact us immediately at privacy@logdit.app.

If a personal data breach affects you, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours of becoming aware of it where required, and notify you directly without undue delay where there is a high risk to your rights and freedoms.

13. Marketing and cookies

The LOGDit mobile app does not use cookies or any other tracking technologies. Our website (logdit.app) uses only strictly necessary cookies to make the site work — no analytics, no advertising and no third-party tracking. The site loads font files from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com); when your browser fetches these, your IP address is transmitted to Google. Google does not set cookies for font requests and we do not exchange any other data with Google through these requests.

We will only send you product or marketing emails if you have given us your consent. You can unsubscribe at any time using the link in any marketing email, or by emailing privacy@logdit.app. We will always send transactional emails (sign-in codes, security notices, service announcements, account deletion confirmations) regardless of marketing preferences.

14. Children

The Service is intended for medical professionals in training and is not directed at anyone under 18. We do not knowingly collect personal data from anyone under 13 (the consent age threshold under the UK Data Protection Act 2018) and will delete any such data if we discover it.

15. Patient information — your responsibility

When you record a case, you are responsible for ensuring that your recording complies with your professional, employer and information-governance obligations (including GMC Confidentiality: good practice in handling patient information and any NHS Trust or practice policy that applies to you). Our redaction pipeline reduces the risk that patient identifiers reach our processors, but it is not infallible. Please do not record in clinical environments where doing so would breach local policy.
