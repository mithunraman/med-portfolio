Assuming the goal is an **MVP that is genuinely useful for UK trainee portfolio work**, not just a polished prototype, the biggest missing features are the ones that improve **portfolio quality, trust, submission readiness, and longitudinal progression**.

Your current app is already strong on **capture, analysis workflow, entry review, and PDP execution**. What is still missing is mostly the layer that makes users feel: “this is safe to rely on for my training.”

---

# Missing features by impact

## High impact

### 1. Curriculum / capability coverage dashboard

The app currently maps capabilities at entry level, but the user still needs a way to answer:

- What capabilities have I covered?
- What areas are weak?
- What is under-evidenced?
- What should I log next?

This is one of the most important missing features because PortfolioPulse is not only about creating individual entries. It should help users manage their **whole portfolio trajectory**. Without a coverage view, users may still end up with a pile of entries but poor curriculum spread.

Why this matters:

- turns the app from entry generator into portfolio manager
- reduces ARCP anxiety
- helps users prioritize future logs and PDPs
- creates a real strategic advantage over simple reflection tools

A good version would include:

- competency heatmap or coverage summary
- count of entries per capability
- underrepresented domains
- suggested next evidence areas

---

### 2. Entry editing / refinement before finalisation

From your feature list, the user can view structured reflections, inspect evidence, and finalize. But it is not obvious that they can directly edit the generated reflection text, title, capability mapping rationale, or key structured fields before completion.

That is a major gap because users need editorial control over portfolio evidence. Even excellent AI-generated draft content will often need:

- wording adjustments
- removal of vague phrasing
- specialty-specific nuance
- tone correction for training portfolio expectations

Why this matters:

- increases trust in AI-generated content
- makes entries feel owned by the doctor, not imposed by the system
- improves final portfolio quality
- reduces the risk of users abandoning generated outputs and rewriting elsewhere

Without this, the app risks feeling like a black box.

---

### 3. Explicit submission-readiness / quality check

You already have workflow states, but what seems missing is a clear “is this entry good enough?” check.

Users need confidence that an entry is not just complete in the app, but is **portfolio-usable**. A readiness layer could assess:

- missing context
- weak reflection
- shallow evidence
- unsupported capability links
- missing PDP review dates
- incomplete sections

Why this matters:

- directly supports the app’s core promise of reducing admin burden
- replaces uncertainty with confidence
- encourages better portfolio habits
- reduces supervisor pushback from poor-quality entries

This is one of the highest leverage features because users care less about whether the app generated text and more about whether that text is acceptable.

---

### 4. Export / share / copy into portfolio system

Right now the app seems strong as an internal workflow, but the doctor will eventually need to use the output in the real world.

Unless PortfolioPulse becomes the portfolio of record, users need a way to move outputs into existing systems. Even a simple export layer would be high impact:

- copy formatted reflection
- copy capability evidence
- export entry summary
- export PDP summary
- PDF or DOCX style output later

Why this matters:

- closes the loop from capture to practical use
- prevents users from doing duplicate work
- makes the app immediately useful even without system integrations
- increases retention because the app produces portable value

Without export, the app risks being “helpful but incomplete.”

---

### 5. Supervisor / review mode

Your app is currently trainee-centered, which is right for MVP, but there is no clear mode for supervisor-facing review or discussion.

Even if supervisors are not primary users yet, the trainee still needs to prepare content that can be reviewed, discussed, and defended. A lightweight review mode could include:

- concise summary view
- linked evidence per capability
- reflection + PDP rationale
- progress snapshot

Why this matters:

- aligns the app with real educational workflows
- increases the credibility of outputs
- helps users prepare for meetings, reviews, and ARCP conversations
- strengthens the app’s value beyond personal note-taking

This does not need to be multi-user collaboration yet. Even a “supervisor-ready view” would be high value.

---

### 6. Safer data handling features for sensitive clinical content

You already have privacy messaging, which is good, but the actual feature layer appears thin from the user point of view. For a clinical documentation-adjacent app, users need stronger trust signals and controls.

Examples:

- explicit warning not to include identifiable patient data
- review-and-redact step before finalisation
- clearer provenance of audio/transcript processing
- delete conversation / delete artefact controls
- stronger guest-mode data warnings

Why this matters:

- trust is existential in this product category
- users will hesitate to use voice capture without safety cues
- reduces compliance and adoption risk
- supports responsible behavior without relying on memory alone

This is especially important because your app encourages quick capture, which raises the risk of oversharing raw details.

---

## Medium impact

### 7. Search across entries, conversations, and goals

Lists and filters are good, but search becomes important once data grows.

Why this matters:

- saves time when looking for prior cases or goals
- supports re-use of past learning
- makes the app scalable beyond early usage
- helps users prepare for appraisal and review conversations

Useful search modes:

- by keyword
- by capability
- by entry type
- by status
- by date range

---

### 8. Better entry metadata structure

Your entry detail screen is strong, but it is not clear whether each entry has structured metadata such as:

- date of event
- clinical setting
- case/procedure/leadership/teaching category
- urgency/complexity
- patient group/context tags

Why this matters:

- improves findability
- improves curriculum mapping quality
- improves export usefulness
- makes coverage analysis much stronger

This is less visible than chat or PDP, but highly valuable over time.

---

### 9. Notification / reminder system

You already surface “goals due soon,” but users may need more proactive nudging:

- review date reminders
- stale draft reminders
- “you haven’t logged in X days”
- incomplete entry reminders

Why this matters:

- supports habit formation
- prevents PDP and draft decay
- increases long-term retention
- helps the app become part of routine practice

This is meaningful, but not as core as coverage, editing, and export.

---

### 10. Duplicate / template support for goals and entries beyond current duplication

You already support “Duplicate to Review” for entries, which is good. A next step would be reusable templates or common structures:

- reflection starters
- goal templates
- action templates
- specialty-specific prompts

Why this matters:

- reduces repeated cognitive effort
- speeds up consistent documentation
- helps weaker reflectors produce better content

---

### 11. Better conversation-to-entry explainability

You already have evidence modals and provenance, which is great. But there may still be room for a more explicit “how this was generated” layer:

- source messages used
- missing assumptions
- confidence warnings
- what the AI inferred vs what the user explicitly said

Why this matters:

- increases trust
- helps users edit more intelligently
- reduces black-box feeling
- particularly helpful for training-related defensibility

---

### 12. Goal outcomes / impact tracking

Right now the PDP side looks operational, but not fully evaluative. You can complete actions and write a completion reflection, but there is still room for measuring:

- was the goal achieved?
- what changed in practice?
- what evidence supports impact?
- should this lead to a new follow-on goal?

Why this matters:

- strengthens the educational quality of PDP
- helps avoid box-ticking
- supports reflective closure

---

## Low impact

### 13. Collaboration / shared review comments

This could eventually matter, but for MVP it is not essential unless the product strategy includes supervisors as active users.

Why lower impact now:

- adds complexity fast
- permissions and workflow get harder
- current value can be proven without it

Still useful later for:

- supervisor comments
- educational meeting prep
- peer review

---

### 14. Rich analytics / streaks / motivational gamification

Things like weekly stats, streaks, and completion charts can help engagement, but they are not central to portfolio value.

Why lower impact:

- nice retention layer, not core workflow
- should come after trust and readiness features

---

### 15. Advanced customization / branding / themes

Helpful, but not strategically important at this stage.

---

### 16. Offline-first drafting

This could be valuable for clinical users, but it is probably lower priority than export, editing, trust, and coverage unless you know your users frequently document with poor connectivity.

---

# Best-priority shortlist

If I had to pick the most important missing features to build next, in order, I would choose:

1. **Curriculum / capability coverage dashboard**
2. **Entry editing and refinement controls**
3. **Submission-readiness / quality check**
4. **Export / share / copy to real portfolio workflow**
5. **Safety and data-control features**
6. **Search across all content**
7. **Reminder system for goals and incomplete entries**

That set would most improve the product’s real-world usefulness.
