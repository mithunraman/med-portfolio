In this context, **PDP actions** are the specific steps in a **Personal Development Plan** that a registrar puts into their portfolio to address a learning need or development goal. The purpose of the PDP is to show that the trainee can identify a need, plan how to meet it, and then review whether it was achieved. ([Royal College of General Practitioners][1])

So a PDP entry usually has:

- **the learning need or goal**,
- **the action(s)** the trainee will take,
- **a timescale/deadline**,
- **the intended outcome/evidence**, and
- later, a **review of completion/progress**. ([GMC UK][2])

In plain English, the **“actions”** are the “what I will do next” part. They should usually be **specific and practical**, often written in a SMART-style way rather than as vague aims. Examples might be: attend a diabetes clinic, complete a workplace-based assessment on ward rounds, read a guideline and discuss it with a supervisor, or get observed performing a procedure. ([East of England][3])

So:

- **Goal:** “Improve confidence managing acute upper GI bleeding.”
- **PDP actions:** “Review the current guideline this week; discuss 2 cases with my consultant this month; complete a CBD on one relevant admission by the end of the placement.”
  That is the kind of thing supervisors expect to see in a registrar portfolio. ([Royal College of General Practitioners][1])
