I want to explore a new product idea: a banjo learning app for users in the UK.

Please help me brainstorm this from a product discovery perspective. Assume this is an early-stage idea with no existing users, no current product, and no validated demand yet.

Focus on:

1. Target users
   - Who might want to learn banjo in the UK?
   - What user segments could exist, such as absolute beginners, folk musicians, guitarists switching instruments, older learners, bluegrass fans, or music teachers?

2. User problems and motivations
   - What pains, goals, anxieties, and motivations might these users have?
   - Why might existing learning options such as YouTube, private lessons, books, or generic music apps not fully solve their needs?

3. Market and context
   - What makes the UK market distinct for banjo learning?
   - Are there relevant music communities, genres, cultural factors, or regional opportunities to consider?

4. Product opportunities
   - What app features could create real value?
   - Consider lesson paths, practice tracking, tuning support, song libraries, feedback tools, community features, teacher access, and UK-specific content.

5. Differentiation
   - How could this app stand out from YouTube, Fender Play, Yousician-style apps, private tutors, and generic online music courses?

6. Assumptions and risks
   - What are the riskiest assumptions behind this idea?
   - Which assumptions should be tested first?

7. Validation plan
   - Suggest low-cost ways to validate demand before building the app.
   - Include customer interview questions, landing page tests, prototype ideas, and possible success metrics.

8. MVP recommendation
   - Recommend a focused MVP for the first version.
   - Explain what should be included, what should be excluded, and why.

Please structure the response clearly using headings, bullet points, and prioritised recommendations. Be practical, critical, and specific rather than overly optimistic.
